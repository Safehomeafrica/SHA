Livelihoods Empowerment Program

Empowering refugee youth and families with skills, resources, and opportunities for economic independence and resilience.

Program Overview

The Livelihoods Empowerment Program is dedicated to equipping refugee youth and families in Nyumanzi Settlement with the skills, resources, and opportunities needed to achieve economic independence and resilience. We focus on vocational training, entrepreneurship, and sustainable income-generating activities. Our approach is holistic, supporting both individuals and groups to build sustainable livelihoods and break the cycle of dependency.

  • Hands-on training in tailoring, carpentry, agriculture, ICT, and more
  • Business incubation and mentorship for start-ups
  • Access to micro-grants and revolving funds
  • Support for women and youth-led enterprises
  • Community-based agricultural projects and cooperatives
  • Market linkage and value addition initiatives

Key Features

  • Vocational skills training (tailoring, carpentry, agriculture, ICT, etc.)
  • Business start-up support and micro-grants
  • Financial literacy and savings groups
  • Mentorship and coaching for young entrepreneurs
  • Market linkage and cooperative development
  • Support for women-led and youth-led enterprises
  • Community-based agricultural projects
  • Digital skills and e-commerce training
  • Ongoing monitoring and evaluation for sustainability
  • Access to local and regional markets
  • Training in sustainable farming techniques
  • Support for persons with disabilities in economic activities
  • Entrepreneurship bootcamps and innovation labs
  • Networking events for youth and women entrepreneurs
  • Mobile money and digital finance training
  • Community cooperatives for bulk purchasing and sales

Program Impact

  • Over 200 youth trained in various vocational skills
  • 50+ small businesses launched by program participants
  • 30% increase in household income among beneficiaries
  • Active women and youth savings groups established
  • Improved food security through agricultural initiatives
  • Increased digital literacy and online business participation

Success Stories

Sarah's Journey: After completing tailoring training, Sarah started her own business and now employs two other refugee women, providing school uniforms to the community.
ICT for Youth: Our ICT training enabled a group of young people to launch a digital services cooperative, offering graphic design and computer literacy classes.
Agro-Enterprise: A youth group received seeds and training, and now supplies fresh vegetables to local markets, improving both income and nutrition.
Digital Leap: Through e-commerce training, a group of young entrepreneurs now sell handmade crafts online, reaching customers beyond the settlement.
